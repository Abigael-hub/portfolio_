About

Analyst turned
evaluation engineer

I spent seven years as a researcher and data analyst at Dalberg Research and NielsenIQ, building evaluation frameworks and data pipelines for the World Bank, IFC, Coca-Cola, and Safaricom. Rigour was not optional — you either caught errors before they reached clients, or you faced the consequences.

When AI moved into production, I recognised that what I had been doing for years — validating outputs, auditing against ground truth, building scoring frameworks — was exactly what AI development needed most. The transition was not a leap. It was a natural extension.

I now contribute to US-based AI evaluation projects remotely, designing benchmark tasks, scoring model outputs, and classifying failure modes for research teams. My edge is the combination of engineering depth and domain expertise in economics and research methodology that pure technical backgrounds rarely bring.
